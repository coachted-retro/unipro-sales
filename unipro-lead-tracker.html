Philadelphia + Bucks County, PA

Philly eCLIPSE permit search Hottest

L&I publishes daily permit and zoning reports. A new mechanical, fire suppression, or commercial alteration permit at a food address = a kitchen being built or renovated right now — they need hood fab, install, and a suppression contractor before opening.

The playMonday morning: pull the daily reports for the past week, filter permit type Mechanical / Fire Suppression, cross-check the address on Atlas. Call the GC or owner on the permit. You're often first in.

Bucks County health dept + township permits Local edge

Bucks County Health Department licenses every eating establishment; townships (Falls, Bristol, Bensalem, Middletown) issue the building permits for new kitchens. Smaller offices, less competition watching them.

The playRight-to-Know request to the county for new food facility licenses issued in the last 90 days, repeated quarterly. Introduce yourself to township code officers — they know every kitchen going in before anyone else.

North + Central New Jersey

NJ Life Hazard Use registrations Hottest

NJ law requires every restaurant/commercial kitchen to register with the state as a Life Hazard Use and get inspected on a state-mandated cycle. This is a state-maintained list of literally every business that must buy what UniPro sells.

The playOPRA request to municipal fire prevention bureaus (or NJ Division of Fire Safety) for current LHU registrations — eating establishment types — in target towns. Also request recent violation/penalty notices: a cited kitchen needs a vendor this week, not this quarter.
DCA Fire Safety portal NJ Division of Fire Safety

NJ DCA Construction Reporter Early radar

Monthly permit and certificate-of-occupancy data from every municipality in NJ, broken out by retail/commercial. Shows you which towns have new commercial square footage coming online — where to aim OPRA requests and field days.

The playMonthly: scan retail new-construction by county (Middlesex, Monmouth, Somerset, Union, Bergen, Morris). Spike in a town → call that town's construction office and fire prevention bureau the same week.
Construction Reporter

County health departments — retail food licenses New openings

In NJ, retail food establishment licensing runs through county/municipal health departments. New license = new kitchen = first suppression inspection coming due fast.

The playQuarterly OPRA to target county health departments for food licenses issued in the last 90 days. New owners haven't picked a fire protection vendor yet — call within their first month.

Hood cleaning companies Best referral source

Hood cleaners are inside your target kitchens every 30–90 days and see every expired suppression tag. Most don't do suppression work — they're a perfect two-way referral partner (you refer cleaning, they refer suppression/extinguisher work).

The playBuild 3–5 reciprocal partnerships with IKECA-member cleaners covering your territory. Offer a simple deal: every referred job that closes gets a referral back or a fee.
IKECA member directory

Restaurant equipment dealers + kitchen GCs Upstream

Whoever sells the cooking line or builds the kitchen knows about the project months before permits close. Hood fabrication is your wedge — dealers need a fab/install partner to quote complete packages.

The playVisit dealers in Philly and along the NJ Turnpike corridor with the UniPro one-pager. Pitch: "Quote your hood, suppression, and extinguisher package through one sub."

Restaurant brokers + commercial realtors Ownership changes

Every restaurant sale or new lease triggers re-inspection, often re-permitting, and a new owner with no vendor loyalty. Brokers love having a fire protection contact who makes their deals close faster.

The playConnect with 2–3 restaurant business brokers per region. Offer free pre-sale compliance walk-throughs — you get first shot at every deficiency found.
