🏗️ How the family is positioned — two distinct clusters
Not every Termac family company belongs in the same conversation. Understanding which companies work together — and why — is the difference between a confusing pitch and a compelling one. There are two distinct clusters, and keeping them clear makes every sale easier.
CLUSTER 1 — The Kitchen Safety System
Universal Fire Protection Quality III ALLPro Stainless Filter Man
UniPro and Quality III are the same service line in different territories — both fire protection, both run on this platform. Together with these other two companies, they serve the same physical system — the commercial kitchen exhaust hood. ALLPro fabricates and installs the custom stainless hood structure. UniPro installs and inspects the suppression system that lives inside it. Filter Man maintains the grease filters that keep it safe and compliant. They are not three separate services — they are three phases of the same project, delivered by three companies that should always be introduced together.
HOW A PROJECT COMES TOGETHER
PHASE 1 — BUILD
ALLPro
Fabricates and installs the custom stainless hood, back walls, and suppression housing to spec. Sized to the cooking line, built to code.
→
PHASE 2 — PROTECT
UniPro
Installs the suppression system inside the hood. On completion, locks in a 3-year inspection contract — semi-annual inspections, NFPA 17A compliant, fully documented.
→
PHASE 3 — MAINTAIN
Filter Man
Sets up a scheduled filter exchange program — monthly or quarterly based on cooking volume. Delivers, swaps, and documents every change for the health inspector.
THE 3-YEAR CONTRACT LOCK-IN — THE MOST IMPORTANT PITCH IN THE ROOM
Every suppression system installation should close with a 3-year inspection contract on the same signature. The customer just spent money on the system — they're already bought in on compliance. The 3-year contract is the natural next step, not an upsell. Frame it simply:

"We install it, we maintain it. Semi-annual inspections, full documentation, one phone call for anything that comes up. Three-year agreement locks in your rate and keeps you in compliance automatically."

That contract is recurring revenue from day one. At $600–$1,200 per account per year, 50 contracts on a 3-year term is $30K–$60K of locked annual RMR — before a single renewal call is made. And at the 34–42× multiple, that contract book is worth $1M–$2.5M in enterprise value. The inspection contract is not the afterthought — it is the business model.
THE FILTER PITCH — SAME VISIT, SAME SIGNATURE
The moment the suppression system install is complete, the rep looks at the hood filters and says: "We can set up a filter exchange program for you at the same time — monthly delivery and swap, compliance documentation included. One more thing you never have to think about." The customer just said yes to a $600/year inspection contract. A $50–$100/month filter program is the easiest add-on sale in the building. Close them together. One project, three revenue streams, one relationship.
CLUSTER 2 — The Kitchen Operations Partner
Termac Chemical Routes G.T.O. Grease Traps
Termac and G.T.O. serve the same commercial kitchens but through a completely different relationship — operational consumables and recurring maintenance rather than safety systems and fabrication. Termac manages the dish machine and chemical program; G.T.O. handles the grease trap compliance. These are day-to-day operational services, not project-based. The relationship is built on route consistency, product reliability, and the trust that comes from showing up on the same schedule every month.
HOW TERMAC CONNECTS TO CLUSTER 1 — WITHOUT BEING CONFUSED WITH IT
The connection is the customer — not the product. When UniPro closes a suppression contract at a new restaurant, that kitchen also needs a dish machine program and grease trap service. The Termac route rep gets a warm introduction: "We already handle your fire protection — our sister company Termac does the dish machine chemicals. Want me to have them reach out?" That's a referral, not a bundle. The customer keeps two separate vendor relationships — UniPro/ALLPro/Filter Man for safety, Termac for operations — but both relationships flow through the Termac family. One territory. One trusted name. Two revenue streams per account.
WHY THIS DISTINCTION MATTERS TO MANAGEMENT
KITCHEN SAFETY CLUSTER
Project-based sales with long-term inspection contracts. One install generates 3–5 years of recurring inspection revenue plus a monthly filter program. RMR compounds from every new system installed. The acquisition and fire alarm expansion strategy lives here. Managed as a single entity: UniPro & Quality III lead, ALLPro and Filter Man close alongside.
Today, this cluster is one person. The platform, the harvester, the AI roadmap — all of it runs through that one person.
KITCHEN OPERATIONS CLUSTER
Route-based sales with monthly recurring revenue. Every account is a regular delivery on a fixed schedule. Revenue is volume-driven — more accounts, more chemical, more revenue. Cross-sells into the safety cluster via warm introductions. Managed separately with its own route optimization, inventory tracking, and upsell intelligence. Termac is the anchor; G.T.O. complements it.
Today, this cluster is six to eight people.
The Termac family doesn't sell a bundle — it sells a relationship. Customers choose which services they need. The family ensures that whatever they need, there is a Termac company ready to provide it. That is the competitive position no single-service competitor can replicate.
